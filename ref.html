Understanding Quality Indices

Learn about the metrics we use to measure environmental health

Air Quality Index (AQI)

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a numerical scale used to communicate how polluted the air currently is or how polluted it is forecast to become. It measures major air pollutants including particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10), ozone (O3), nitrogen dioxide (NO2), sulfur dioxide (SO2), and carbon monoxide (CO).

AQI Categories:

  • 0-50: Good
  • 51-100: Moderate
  • 101-150: Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups
  • 151-200: Unhealthy
  • 201-300: Very Unhealthy
  • 301-500: Hazardous

Water Quality Index (WQI)

The Water Quality Index (WQI) is a tool that provides a composite influence of individual water quality parameters on the overall quality of water. It considers parameters like pH, dissolved oxygen (DO), biochemical oxygen demand (BOD), temperature, turbidity, total dissolved solids (TDS), nitrates, and fecal coliform.

WQI Categories:

  • 90-100: Excellent
  • 70-90: Good
  • 50-70: Medium
  • 25-50: Bad
  • 0-25: Very Bad

About AQI

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is an index for reporting daily air quality. It tells you how clean or polluted your air is, and what associated health effects might be a concern for you. The AQI focuses on health effects you may experience within a few hours or days after breathing polluted air.

Our AQI prediction model analyzes historical air quality data along with meteorological factors to forecast future AQI values with high accuracy. The model considers parameters like PM2.5, PM10, O3, NO2, SO2, CO, temperature, humidity, and wind speed.

Required CSV Format:

Your CSV file should include the following columns (in any order):

  • Date (YYYY-MM-DD format)
  • PM2.5 (µg/m³)
  • PM10 (µg/m³)
  • O3 (ppb)
  • NO2 (ppb)
  • SO2 (ppb)
  • CO (ppm)
  • Temperature (°C)
  • Humidity (%)
  • Wind Speed (km/h)

About WQI

The Water Quality Index (WQI) is a valuable tool for water quality assessment. It provides a single number (like a grade) that expresses overall water quality at a certain location and time based on several water quality parameters. The WQI considers parameters that affect water quality such as dissolved oxygen, pH, turbidity, dissolved solids, nitrates, and fecal coliform.

Our WQI prediction model analyzes historical water quality data along with environmental factors to forecast future WQI values with high accuracy. The model considers parameters like pH, dissolved oxygen (DO), biochemical oxygen demand (BOD), temperature, turbidity, total dissolved solids (TDS), nitrates, and fecal coliform.

Required CSV Format:

Your CSV file should include the following columns (in any order):

  • Date (YYYY-MM-DD format)
  • pH
  • Dissolved Oxygen (mg/L)
  • Biochemical Oxygen Demand (mg/L)
  • Temperature (°C)
  • Turbidity (NTU)
  • Total Dissolved Solids (mg/L)
  • Nitrates (mg/L)
  • Fecal Coliform (CFU/100mL)
  • Conductivity (µS/cm)
